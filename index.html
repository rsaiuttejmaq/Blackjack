Blackjack Rules

Blackjack is a popular card game typically played in casinos. The objective is to beat the dealer by having a hand value as close to 21 as possible without exceeding it. Here are the basic rules:

Card Values

  • Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10 points.
  • Aces can be worth either 1 point or 11 points, depending on which value benefits the hand more.

Gameplay

  1. The game starts with each player receiving two cards. The dealer also receives two cards, with one card face up (the "upcard") and one card face down (the "hole card").
  2. Players can choose to "hit" (take an additional card) or "stand" (keep their current hand).
  3. Players can continue to hit until they choose to stand or exceed 21 points (bust).
  4. If a player busts, they lose the round automatically.
  5. Once all players have finished, the dealer reveals their hole card and must hit until they reach at least 17 points.
  6. If the dealer busts, all remaining players win. If not, hands are compared to determine the winner.

Winning

  • If your hand is closer to 21 than the dealer's, you win and are paid 1:1.
  • If you get a Blackjack (an Ace and a 10-point card) on your first two cards, you typically win 1.5 times your bet.
  • If the dealer's hand beats yours, you lose your bet.
  • If you tie with the dealer, it's a "push," and you neither win nor lose your bet.
